General information

The worldwide climate change is an undeniable factor in the everyday life. The rising sea levels, more extreme weather conditions and global warming do have different causes: examples of this are biotic processes, variations in solar radiation and plate tectonics. However, especially human activities have been identified as a significant cause of this changes. One of the largest factors in this are the rises in greenhouse gasses. Carbon dioxide (CO2) is one of these greenhouse gasses that increased enormously since the Industrial Revolution. Not only the bigger use of factories or transport lead to this change, but also the gradual population shift from rural to urban areas, also known as urbanization. This urbanization led to an enlargement of the energy consumption in many countries, since people in cities had a wider access to facilities that produced a lot of carbon dioxide (Dhakal, 2010).
